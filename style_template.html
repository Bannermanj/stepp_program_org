How much does college cost?

In 2014-2015, the average yearly total for tuition/fees and room/board for full-time undergraduate students was $18,943 for public 4-year institutions and $42,419 for private 4-year institutions. At 2-year institutions, the total for tuition/fees (not including room/board) was $3,347 for public schools. These totals are for one academic year.

(Source: College Board http://trends.collegeboard.org/college-pricing/figures-tables/ average-published-undergraduate-charges-sector-2014-15)

How do people pay for college?

  • Parents and/or students save money ahead of time
  • Scholarships and/or grants (do not need to be paid back; may be restrictions on receiving them)
  • Student loans and/or parent loans (must be paid back with interest)
  • Work-study or other student employment

Parents Chime In

Some students understand this point better when given this explanation.

Te achers vs. Professors: There is a reason that college instructors are generally called professors instead of teachers. In high school, your teachers were responsible for directly teaching you things and making sure that you learned them. That’s why they’re called teachers. To “profess” something means to declare or announce it. In college, the professor is only responsible for announcing the information. It’s up to you as the student to actually do what it takes to learn it. That’s why they’re called professors.
